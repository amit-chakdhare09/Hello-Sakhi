Is someone blackmailing you ?

Blackmail involves using threats or coercion to obtain something of value from another person. This can include money , images, videos, property, or other forms of compensation.

Here's what you should do:

  • Don't engage: Avoid any further communication with the blackmailer.

  • Document everything: Keep a record of all communications, including emails, texts, and voicemails.

  • Seek legal advice: Consult with a lawyer who specializes in blackmail or extortion.

  • Report to the authorities: Contact your local police or a cybercrime police unit in your city.

Victim of Sexual or domestic Assault or Rape

Sexual Assault: Sexual assault is any unwanted sexual contact or behavior that occurs without explicit consent. It can range from inappropriate touching to more severe acts, causing deep emotional and physical trauma to the victim.

Domestic Assault: Domestic assault refers to physical, emotional, or psychological abuse within a domestic setting, such as in a marriage or cohabitation. It often involves power and control dynamics, leaving the victim feeling trapped and vulnerable.

Rape: Rape is a severe form of sexual assault where an individual is forced into non-consensual sexual intercourse. It is a violent crime that deeply affects the survivor's physical, emotional, and mental well-being.

Physical Evidence:

1.Medical Examination : A rape kit can collect evidence such as DNA, bruising, or other injuries.
2.Clothing : The victim's clothing may contain evidence like DNA or fibers.
3.Forensic Evidence : This can include any physical trace left by the perpetrator, such as hair or skin cells.

Witness Testimonies:
1.Victim's Testimony: The victim's account of the incident is crucial.
2.Witnesses: Statements from anyone who may have seen or heard something relevant.

Documentation:
1.Police Reports: Immediate reporting can help create an official record.
2.Medical Records: Documentation of injuries or psychological impact. 3.Text Messages/Emails: Any communication that might support the victim's claim.

Consent:
Evidence or lack thereof regarding consent is central to the case.

Complaint & Investigation Cell, National Commission for Women

The Complaints and Investigation Cell of the Commission processes all the complaints whether received written or suo-motu under Section 10 of the NCW Act. The complaints received relate to domestic violence, harassment, dowry, torture, desertion, bigamy, rape, refusal to register FIR, cruelty by husband, deprivation, gender discrimination and sexual harassment at work place.


SHe-Box : Platform to register cases (Will start operating from October 2024)

Government of India's Sexual Harassment Electronic Box (SHe Box):
The Government of India's Sexual Harassment Electronic Box (SHE Box) is an online platform where women can file complaints regarding sexual harassment at the workplace.

How to Do:

Register : The first step is to register on the SHE Box platform.
File a Complaint Once registered, women can file a complaint regarding sexual harassment at the workplace.
Track the Status : The status of the complaint can be tracked online, providing transparency and ease of use.
